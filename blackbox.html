Black Box Investigation!!

At Engineering And Science University Magnet School otherwise known as ESUMS , we have a class called New Media and Tech for Social Change. In this class there is a mystery black box. No student knew what was inside it. A big briefcase looking box that had a funnel on top, with a single tube that acted as a faucet. You would put water inside the box, it would either allow you to get the same amount of water, less or even none! In regards to this black box, we performed multiple test trials, while answers varied, we were able to grasp a small concept of its contents. With that, we used multiple programs like Photoshop and MAYA, a program Disney uses!

As well as using programs, performed trials on the actual black box. While most of the results varied, for example on trial 4, no water came out despite putting in 1000ml. But for trial 5, 370ml came out despite putting in 1000ml.

Claim

Based on this, I believe that inside it there is a funnel inside connecting 3 separate tubes. The first one being a normal plastic tube, allowing it to release the same amount of water that was put in. The second tube would be a normal plastic tube except inside it, there would be a small sponge, this sponge would allow some water to be absorbed, the last tube would be blocked off on the end, allowing for no water to leave.

Evidence 1

Some evidence I have is the fact we also made our own mini version of the black box in groups. We made our version using a cardboard box, a plastic tube, half of a plastic tube, a small funnel, hot glue and tape. We ended up doing our own trials, while our cardboard boxes did not work as well as the real Black box, it let us use programs like MAYA, giving us a chance to animate it in a 3d format.

Evidence 2

Using a program: MAYA we animated our black box. These are some of the tools we used, as well as the results! Another program we used was Adobe photoshop. Using that, we learned about the rule of thirds. The rule of thirds mentions how things look better when they're slightly off center.

We also photographed and made our own mini versions. We used supplies like cardboard and PVC pipes in order to model our vision of the black box's interior. We used Rule of thirds in photography as well.
